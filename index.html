About M&M Candies

M&M's are colorful button-shaped chocolates produced by Mars, Incorporated. The candy-coated chocolate pieces are widely recognized for their vibrant colors and distinctive 'm' logo.

Introduced in 1941, M&M's have become one of the most popular and iconic candy brands worldwide. Originally created for military rations during World War II, they quickly gained popularity among civilians and evolved into the beloved treat we know today.

Nutritional Information

Each serving of M&M's typically contains a mix of milk chocolate, a colorful candy shell, and comes in various flavors such as peanut, crispy, and more. While delicious, it's essential to enjoy them in moderation. Be mindful of the nutritional content, including calories, sugar, and fat.
